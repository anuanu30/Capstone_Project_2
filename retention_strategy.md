# Customer Retention Strategy

## Objective

The objective of this strategy is to identify customer groups requiring retention attention before deploying predictive churn models. The strategy combines RFM analysis with behavioural indicators such as returns, discount usage, ratings, and delivery experience.

---

# Segment-wise Retention Strategy

## 1. Champions

### Behaviour
- High purchase frequency
- High monetary contribution
- Recent active customers

### Retention Action
- VIP loyalty rewards
- Early access to products
- Personalized premium offers
- Referral programs

### Expected Business Value
Protecting this segment helps maintain a stable revenue base and encourages brand advocacy.

### Priority
High

---

## 2. Loyal Customers

### Behaviour
- Frequent repeat purchases
- Consistent engagement
- Moderate-to-high spending

### Retention Action
- Cross-sell recommendations
- Loyalty point incentives
- Subscription or membership offers

### Expected Business Value
Strong opportunity for upselling and long-term retention.

### Priority
Medium-High

---

## 3. At Risk Customers

### Behaviour
- Previously valuable customers
- Reduced recent activity
- Increasing inactivity

### Retention Action
- Personalized win-back campaigns
- Limited-time discount offers
- Reminder notifications
- Feedback collection campaigns

### Expected Business Value
Recovering existing customers is cheaper than acquiring new customers.

### Priority
Highest

---

## 4. High Return Customers

### Behaviour
- Frequent product returns
- Potential dissatisfaction
- Possible product-quality or expectation mismatch

### Retention Action
- Customer support outreach
- Product recommendation refinement
- Faster issue resolution
- Return-reason analysis

### Expected Business Value
Reducing return behaviour can improve both customer satisfaction and profitability.

### Priority
High

---

## 5. Dormant Customers

### Behaviour
- Long inactive period
- Very low engagement

### Retention Action
- Low-cost email campaigns
- Re-engagement promotions
- Seasonal campaigns

### Expected Business Value
Low recovery probability but possible low-cost reactivation.

### Priority
Low

---

## 6. New Customers

### Behaviour
- Recently acquired customers
- Limited purchase history

### Retention Action
- Onboarding communication
- Personalized recommendations
- First-purchase follow-up offers

### Expected Business Value
Improves early customer retention and increases repeat purchase probability.

### Priority
Medium

---

# Campaign Budget Prioritization

Given a limited retention budget, the following prioritization strategy is recommended:

| Priority Rank | Segment | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | At Risk Customers | High recovery value and lower acquisition cost |
| 2 | Champions | Critical revenue contributors |
| 3 | High Return Customers | Prevent dissatisfaction-driven churn |
| 4 | Loyal Customers | Upsell and engagement opportunities |
| 5 | New Customers | Improve early retention |
| 6 | Dormant Customers | Lowest ROI probability |

---

# Business Recommendation

The company should focus primarily on retaining high-value customers showing early signs of disengagement. Combining RFM metrics with behavioural signals provides a more accurate understanding of customer risk and engagement patterns.
